Indonesia's Personal Data Protection (PDP) Law (Law No. 27/2022) is ASEAN's most comprehensive data privacy regulation. Unlike earlier sectoral rules, it creates blanket obligations for all companies handling personal data — regardless of industry or origin.

⚠️ Critical: Enforcement is ramping up in 2024-2025. The authority (BPJI) will start audits and investigations. Non-compliance can result in fines up to 2% of annual revenue + criminal penalties for executives.

For Your Organization: Three Critical Questions

Q1: Will I face fines for non-compliance?

Yes. Up to 2% of annual revenue for administrative violations. Up to IDR 5-6 billion (~$320-400k USD) per executive for criminal violations. Plus civil lawsuits from data subjects.

Q2: What data triggers the strictest rules?

  • Health, genetic, biometric data
  • Criminal records
  • Children's data
  • Financial information
  • Different rules apply to each category

Q3: Do I need to appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO)?

Yes, if: You're processing large-scale data + sensitive personal data. Most Chinese companies operating in Indonesia will need one.

What You Must Do (Non-Negotiable)

Your Situation Your Obligation
Collecting customer data (name, email, phone) Get valid consent before processing. Be transparent about use.
Processing health/biometric/financial data Enhanced security measures required. Breach notification within 30 days.

Employing people in Indonesia Record all employee data processing. Employees have right to access/delete data.
Using AI/automated decision-making Data subjects can object. Cannot make employment/credit decisions on algorithms alone.

Your Core Compliance Checklist

Tier 1: Essential (Do This First)

✓ Define your legal basis for each data collection

Consent? Contract obligation? Legal requirement? Document it.

✓ Conduct a Data Processing Audit

Map all data flows: where does customer data live? Who accesses it? How long do you keep it?

✓ Implement basic security

Access controls, encryption (especially for sensitive data), consider ISO 27001 certification.

✓ Create a Data Protection Policy

Share with employees + customers. Include breach notification + data subject rights procedures.

Tier 2: Medium Priority (Within 6 Months)

  • Conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) — Required if processing involves systematic monitoring or sensitive data at scale.
  • Appoint a Data Protection Officer — Required if: large-scale personal data processing + sensitive data.
  • Establish a Data Breach Response Procedure — Breach notification to authorities within 30 days.

Tier 3: Long-term / As-Needed

  • Cross-border transfer agreements (if you move customer data out of Indonesia)
  • Third-party processor agreements (with payment processors, cloud hosts, etc.)
  • International compliance alignment (PDPA Singapore, PIPL China, CCPA US)

Common Compliance Mistakes for Chinese Companies

Mistake Why It's Costly How to Fix It
No consent before marketing Illegal. Fine up to 2% revenue. Customer backlash. Get explicit opt-in. Document it.
Unclear data retention "We keep data forever" = violation. Set clear retention periods by data type. Delete after purpose ends.
No employee access controls Any employee can view customer data? Violation. Implement role-based access. Audit logs.
Transferring data outside Indonesia without basis Cross-border transfers require specific legal framework. Get legal advice. Consider data residency in Indonesia or verify adequacy.
Not notifying breach within 30 days Criminal liability. Jail time possible. Have 72-hour internal escalation process.
Ignoring data subject rights Customer asks to see their data? Must respond in 30 days. Build access/deletion workflows.

Reality Check: What Happens If You Violate?

Administrative Sanctions

  • Written warning (first offense)
  • Temporary suspension of processing activities
  • Deletion/destruction of unlawfully collected data
  • Fines: Up to 2% of annual revenue (e.g., $10M company = up to $200k)

Criminal Liability

  • Unlawful collection/use of personal data: Up to 5 years imprisonment + IDR 5 billion fine
  • Unauthorized disclosure: Up to 4 years + IDR 4 billion fine
  • Falsification of data: Up to 6 years + IDR 6 billion fine
  • For corporations: Fines up to 10× individual penalties + license suspension/revocation

Civil Liability

  • Data subjects can sue for damages (material + non-material)
  • Class-action lawsuits possible (especially if breach affects many people)
  • Reputational damage + customer churn
